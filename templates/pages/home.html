Correct daily brushing and toothpaste is the most important way to maintain oral and dental hygiene and prevent cavities and the resulting diseases. Toothbrush care is also an important component of a clean and healthy mouth. Dental health is a major element in preserving human health, as well as preventing it, and Teeth Lap play an important role in that.

The importance of brushing teeth:
• Preventing the accumulation of plaque on the teeth (which is a sticky and thin layer formed on the surface of the teeth from food residues and contains bacteria that cause gum disease and tooth decay).
• Prevention of gingivitis.
• Save time and money spent on treating dental problems.
• Get fresh breath, by preventing the formation of bacteria that cause bad odors.
• Reducing the formation of external pigmentation on the surfaces of the teeth.

Teeth cleaning steps:
• Place the brush on the teeth at an angle of 45 degrees, so that the bristles of the brush touch the tip of the gums and teeth.
• Gently brush every 2 or 3 teeth together, back and forth from the gum side to the end of the tooth, and so on for the rest of the teeth.
• Brush the back teeth (molars) by placing the brush at an angle of 45 degrees, back and forth, and in a circular motion on all internal surfaces.
• Clean the inside of the teeth using the brush head, and move the brush longitudinally up and down.
• Brushing the edges of the teeth, then brushing the tongue using its own brush, or with a toothbrush.
